But it is a great game. One could argue that it is just a tower defense game, and just 110 heroes to play with. But no two games are similar and the game always evolves, often involving much more complexity then one could notice. Laning, drafting, team fights, split push, ganks, roshan, map control, warding (bah), etc. all in 30-40 minutes.

Having spent thousands of hours including bot matches and videos (a huge fan of DotaFX true stories). I absolutely hated Anti-mage (worth calling anti-fun) for he is too boring to play with, I agree that Naga-siren is hate able but I respect her for her complexity - this hero produces illusions of herself which needs to be controlled making the hero difficult to use. While I completely fell in love with Meepo, Vengeful Sprit, Skywrath and Storm. Where Invoker and Meepo are creations for inspiration, one should know the game at least for them.

To explain a bit, Invoker has three elements - fire, ice, and storm, and can invoke a spell on the combination (with repetation) of the elements he wants(like more fire, less ice or something). He can have a total of 3 elements at a time, and so he has (3+3-1)!/3!(3-1)! = 10 spells to play with, which gains power as game progresses late. And about Meepo, Meepo multiplies as he levels up. So he starts as 1 and can become 5. Controlling each unit needs a lot of skills and when executed correctly can be devastating for the enemies.

Even more professionals such as Merlini, Iceiceice, Aui_2000, etc. are very inspiring considering their maturity, and perspective. Further DOTA 2 has a wonderful reddit community with it. And Icefrog, the man behind the game and community, is a great person as agreed in We, the community.
