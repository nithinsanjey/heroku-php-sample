Getting the most out of CEG

December 29, 2017
by UVSANKAR

Disclaimer: Consider this article a senior's view of what to do in CEG to be become a better programmer / engineer. I'm by no means a stellar programmer. I'm writing this only based on my observations and experience.

First Year:

If you think securing an admission in CEG will straight away make you a good engineer / land you in a high paying job, Please forget it. Consistent hard work is important.

First Sem:

  • This is the sem, you should get to know all the happenings in the college(Events, Clubs, Scholarships).
  • If you don't know anything about CS please start reading from the Day 1. I would recommend buying a good book on 'C' language( A book by Reema Thareja, maybe !). Don't use local author books throughout your life unless the author is a well known authority on that subject matter.
  • Take the FOC lab seriously.
  • Learn about Linux and some basic shell commands.
  • Visit RCC and Anna Centenary library frequently.

Second Sem:

  • You should be comfortable in writing basic C / C++ (I do know both are completely different paradigms ;P) programmes(Printing patterns,..) by now.
  • Get actively involved in Abacus(Dept. Symposium). Get to know your dept seniors. It will help you a lot.
  • Check out Competitive programming. Start solving CP problems(spoj.com, codechef, codeforces). Solving these problems will improve your problem solving skills.

Second Year

  • By the end of 3rd sem try to solve at-least 150 problems(Don't solve easy problems.)
  • Try to compete in intra college symposiums with an intent to learn not to win.(Sync, I++). You should be able to do reasonably well if you have solved around 150 problems.
  • Learn about GIT.
  • Parallely try to do some pet projects using git for version control.
  • By the end of this year you should be aware of all the basic concepts in CP (binary search, DP, greedy, Graph traversals ,..)
  • Use your semester leaves wisely. Try to contribute to Open source projects. (If possible try to participate in GSOC)

Third Year

  • By this time you should have a pretty good idea of your area of interest in Computer Science(Networks, Competitive programming,.. )
  • Focus on your area of interest.
  • Code Code Code. I cant stress this much. You should be coding daily. Doesn't matter what you code.
  • Focus on your problem solving skill. This will help you to get internships in good companies in the 6th sem.
  • There are many papers you will taking this year(OS, TOC, Compilers, Networks). Please religiously study these things. This will help you throughout your career.
  • Attend all Technical events with an intent to win.

Fourth Year

  • Intentionally left empty - you should be able to figure out what to do by now.
  • There can will be many distractions throughout your four years in CEG. I believe 3rd is the most distractive.
  • Its ok to chill out sometimes but ask your self this - Am I doing anything to become a better engineer ?. If no then seriously reconsider what you are doing.
